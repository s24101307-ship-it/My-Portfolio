Abstract

Statistics are fundamental to the process of decision-making across virtually all disciplines. They provide the essential quantitative foundation for the collection, organization, analysis, and interpretation of data, enabling individuals and institutions to make informed and rational choices. In a modern society increasingly driven by data and technology, statistical knowledge has become indispensable for transforming raw information into meaningful insights that support logical, objective, and evidence-based decision-making. By applying statistical principles, decision-makers can effectively reduce uncertainty, identify patterns and relationships, forecast potential outcomes, and evaluate the impact of their actions in a systematic and empirical manner.
